\chapter{Related Work}
\label{chap:sota}

In this chapter we review the literature on the concrete tasks most directly related to this work. We begin with \acf{ie}, in \Cref{sec:information_extraction}, and then narrow our focus to \acf{ee}, since we concentrate on entities and do not address other \ac{ie} tasks, such as relation extraction. \Ac{ee} tasks include \acf{ner}, \acf{nel}, NIL prediction, and NIL clustering. The latter two address the \nilchallenge, \ie the problem of identifying mentions that refer to previously unseen or NIL entities.

We use the term \acf{kc} to collectively denote \ac{nel}, NIL prediction, and NIL clustering, since these tasks consolidate the extracted knowledge by grouping together mentions of the same entities and thereby support downstream processes such as faceted search~\cite{ARMENTANO2014_nlp_faceted,hearst_2006_faceted}. By contrast, \ac{ner} identifies mentions of entities without determining which of them refer to the same underlying entity.

After reviewing \ac{ee} and \ac{kc}, in \Cref{sec:sota:arch}, we turn to the literature on \aclp{dia} and document management models for the legal domain, and finally to \acf{qa}, with particular attention to \acf{kgqa}, in \Cref{sec:sota:qa}.
