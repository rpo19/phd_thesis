This chapter lays the foundations for the rest of the thesis by carefully introducing the background on which this work builds. In \Cref{sec:back:informationaccess}, we begin by discussing \acl{ia} and the notions of \emph{information needs} and \emph{information objects}~\cite{entityorientedsearch2018balog}. In \Cref{back:entities}, we define what is an entity and how entity-based knowledge is represented in \aclp{kr} and \aclp{kb}. To better situate the discussion within the recent advancements in \ac{nlp} we detail the core concepts behind modern \aclp{llm} in \Cref{sec:rw:llms}. Once this broader context is established, in \Cref{sec:domains}, we describe the Italian legal system and the regulations for using \acl{ai} within it, with some example applications. We then present illustrative examples of an Italian civil judgment and a chat log from an Italian investigation---whose analysis has motivated several works~\cite{BELLANDI2024,aixia_2023,pozzi_wise_2025} produced during my \phd
